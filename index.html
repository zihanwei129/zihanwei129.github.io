Welcome! I'm a PhD student at SIGMA group in Department of Geophysics, Stanford University. I'm also pursuing a PhD minor in computer science. My work focuses on applying numerical modeling and data-model integration to different earth systems, including volcanoes, sustainable agriculture, water resources and pollution, and natural hazards. I'm eager about using my knowledge and skills to pursue our sustainable future, with a harmony of nature and our society.

I was born in Sichuan, China, a place famous for pandas, spicy food and most importantly, gorgeous natural landscape. I've been deeply attracted by earth science since my childhood. Before coming to Stanford, I got my Bachelor's degree in Geology from Peking University, where I established an educated love of our Earth and the resolution of devoting myself to its future.
